Turkish Delight Ice Cream

Rosewater perfection with chocolate coating

Calories 295
Protein 42g
Carbs 24g
Freeze Time 24 hours

Ingredients

  • 420 ml reduced fat high protein milk (A2 Protein Plus or Devondale Protein Plus)
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 5 ml vanilla extract
  • 3 g rosewater (Queen brand - start with less, can't undo!)
  • 2-3 drops red food coloring (optional but makes it look authentic)
  • 22 g vanilla whey protein
  • 40 g erythritol or Natvia
  • 0.5 g salt
  • 0.8 g xanthan gum
  • 20 g dark chocolate, melted with 5g coconut oil (for coating)
  • 10 g pistachios, roughly chopped and frozen (optional)

Instructions

  1. Add milk, egg yolk, vanilla, rosewater (START WITH 3g) to container
  2. Add 2-3 drops red food coloring if using
  3. Blend with immersion blender
  4. Mix dry ingredients separately
  5. Continue blending while adding dry ingredients
  6. Mix 90 seconds, TASTE TEST: Should have noticeable but not overpowering rosewater flavor
  7. If too subtle, add 0.5g more rosewater and blend
  8. Let sit 10 minutes for foam to settle
  9. Pour into pint, cap and freeze 24 hours
  10. Make chocolate coating: Melt dark chocolate with coconut oil, spread very thin on parchment paper, freeze until solid (2 hours), break into irregular shards
  11. Run outside of pint under hot water for 60 seconds before spinning
  12. Spin on Light Ice Cream setting
  13. Make hole near side of pint, add chocolate shards (work FAST), add pistachios if using, push toward bottom, cover with ice cream, run Mix-in setting briefly

Nutrition per Pint

295 Calories
42g Protein
24g Carbs
6g Fat

Pro Tips

  • CRITICAL WARNING: Rosewater is incredibly strong - start with 3g (¾ tsp). Too much rosewater tastes like perfume/soap. You can always add more, but can't remove it!
  • If you've never had Turkish delight, try authentic version first to understand the flavor
  • Pink food coloring is optional but makes it look more authentic
  • Chocolate shards should be thin - they're coating, not chunks
  • This is a "love it or hate it" flavor - rosewater is polarizing
  • Make half recipe first to test if you like rosewater level
  • Adelaide Shopping: Rosewater (Queen brand) at Coles/Woolworths (baking aisle). Pistachios (unsalted, shelled) at Coles/Woolworths
