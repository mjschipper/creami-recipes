Ferrero Rocher Ice Cream

Chocolate hazelnut heaven with wafer crunch

Calories 340
Protein 46g
Carbs 28g
Freeze Time 24 hours

Ingredients

  • 380 ml reduced fat high protein milk (A2 Protein Plus or Devondale Protein Plus)
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 20 g hazelnut butter
  • 5 ml vanilla extract
  • 2 ml hazelnut extract
  • 22 g chocolate whey protein
  • 40 g erythritol or Natvia
  • 8 g cocoa powder
  • 3 drops coffee extract
  • 0.5 g salt
  • 0.8 g xanthan gum
  • 20 g hazelnuts, roughly chopped and frozen
  • 15 g Arnott's Granita biscuits, crushed and frozen
  • 10 g dark chocolate, chopped into small chunks and frozen

Instructions

  1. Prepare mix-ins first: Chop hazelnuts roughly, freeze solid. Crush biscuits into small pieces, freeze solid. Chop dark chocolate into small chunks, freeze solid
  2. Add milk, egg yolk, hazelnut butter, extracts to container
  3. Blend with immersion blender for 60 seconds
  4. Mix dry ingredients separately
  5. Continue blending while adding dry ingredients
  6. Mix 90 seconds after all ingredients added
  7. Let sit 10 minutes for foam to settle
  8. Pour into pint, cap and freeze 24 hours
  9. Run outside of pint under hot water for 60 seconds before spinning
  10. Spin on Light Ice Cream setting
  11. Make hole slightly off-center, layer: hazelnuts, wafer pieces, chocolate chunks, repeat
  12. Push toward bottom (work FAST), cover with ice cream, run Mix-in setting

Nutrition per Pint

340 Calories
46g Protein
28g Carbs
12g Fat

Pro Tips

  • This is ONE recipe where Nutella actually improves it: Instead of hazelnuts, use 15g Nutella swirled through (adds 80 cal but worth it!)
  • Wafer pieces MUST be frozen solid or they dissolve
  • Work very fast during assembly - chocolate melts quickly
  • Adelaide Alternatives: Instead of wafers, Tim Tam crumbs work surprisingly well. For premium, use Haigh's dark chocolate (local Adelaide chocolate!)
  • Layer mix-ins strategically - hazelnuts first, then wafers, then chocolate for best distribution
